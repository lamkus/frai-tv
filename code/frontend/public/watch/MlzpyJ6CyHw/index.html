Der kleine Maulwurf (Czech: Krtek/Krteček, "The Little Mole") is an animated series created by Czech animator and illustrator Zdeněk Miler (1921–2011). The first film, "Jak krtek ke kalhotkám přišel" (How the Mole Got His Trousers), was produced in 1957 at the Bratři v triku studio of Krátký film Praha in Prague, Czechoslovakia, and won a directing award at the Venice Film Festival. Roughly 49–50 short films were made between 1957 and 2002; only the first contains narration, after which Miler used his daughters' wordless exclamations so the cartoons could be understood worldwide. NOTE: Krtek is NOT public domain — it remains under copyright held by Miler's estate (a well-documented heirs' dispute followed his 2011 death), so it should not be labeled as a public-domain work.

Produktion / Production: Bratři v triku studio, Krátký film Praha (Prague Short Film studio), Prague, Czechoslovakia (today Czech Republic). Creator Zdeněk Miler was born in Kladno, near Prague..
