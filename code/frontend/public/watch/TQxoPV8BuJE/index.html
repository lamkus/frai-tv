Casper the Friendly Ghost is a series of theatrical animated shorts produced by Famous Studios (Paramount Pictures' in-house animation division, the successor to Fleischer Studios; renamed Paramount Cartoon Studios in 1956) and distributed by Paramount Pictures. The character was created by Seymour Reit and Joe Oriolo and debuted in the Noveltoon "The Friendly Ghost," released November 16, 1945 and directed by Isadore Sparber. After two further Noveltoons ("There's Good Boos To-Night," 1948; "A Haunting We Will Go," 1949), Casper graduated to his own series, with Paramount releasing dedicated Casper shorts from 1950 through 1959 for a total of 55 theatrical cartoons, making him Paramount's most popular cartoon character after Popeye.
