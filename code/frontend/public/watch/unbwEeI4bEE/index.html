Popeye the Sailor began as a theatrical cartoon series in 1933, when Fleischer Studios (founded by brothers Max and Dave Fleischer, distributed by Paramount Pictures) adapted the character E.C. Segar had created for King Features' "Thimble Theatre" comic strip on January 17, 1929. Fleischer Studios produced 109 Popeye shorts from 1933 to 1942, first at 1600 Broadway in New York City and, from late 1938, at a new studio in Miami, Florida. In 1941-42 Paramount took control and renamed the studio Famous Studios, which produced a further 122 Popeye shorts from 1942 to 1957 (14 black-and-white, then color from "Her Honor the Mare"), ending with "Spooky Swabs" (1957). The early voice of Popeye was William Costello, later and most famously Jack Mercer; Olive Oyl was voiced by Mae Questel.

Produktion / Production: Fleischer Studios, 1600 Broadway, New York City, USA (1933-1938); then Fleischer Studios, Miami, Florida, USA (late 1938-1942); then Famous Studios, Miami, Florida and later New York City, USA (1942-1957). All distributed by Paramount Pictures..
