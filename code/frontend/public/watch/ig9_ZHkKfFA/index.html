Betty Boop was created by animator Grim Natwick at the request of Max Fleischer and produced by Fleischer Studios for distribution by Paramount Pictures. She debuted on August 9, 1930 in "Dizzy Dishes," the sixth entry in the Talkartoon series, and was inspired by the flapper-era singing style of Helen Kane; Mae Questel became her definitive voice from 1931 to 1939. After becoming the studio's breakout star, the Talkartoons were rebranded as her own Betty Boop series in August 1932, totaling roughly 90 theatrical shorts through 1939, many featuring jazz legends like Cab Calloway, Don Redman, and Louis Armstrong. From 1934, enforcement of the Motion Picture Production (Hays) Code toned down her risqué Pre-Code flapper image into a more demure career girl, making the early 1930s shorts prized examples of Pre-Code American animation.
